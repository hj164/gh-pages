Human-Centred Collection Digitalisation (PhD research)

Han Jiang: Design Research & Strategy

Summary

 
My PhD research explores how museums worldwide leverage Human-Centred Design (HCD) to address their digital collection challenges by incorporating stakeholder input and user engagement. By examining the systems and collaborative process that support HCD implementation and its interactions with institutional, cultural, and technological factors, it aims to provide insights into the diverse paths for digitalising museum collections.

Challenges

Many regions lack long-term strategies to promote the growth of digital transformation in museums. Most museums struggle with finding the right collaborators (e.g. technology providers, stakeholders, collection specialists) and ensuring sustainable digital product development.

Goals

The research answers three questions:

  • What strategies, roles, resources, and infrastructures are required for HCD-based collection digitalisation? 
  • What cultural, technological, and institutional factors impact design decisions in the projects?

Mixed Methods

Semi-structured interviews: I conducted 17 in-depth interviews with participants working on various aspects (e.g. UI/UX design, PM, CMS, AI engineering, curation, etc.) of digitalisation projects worldwide. 

Survey: A 21-item survey was designed to validate themes derived from interviews. I am currently collecting responses from the survey.

Tools: Atlas.ti; Qualtrics; Kumu; Figma

Key Insights

My research revealed:

  • Institutional enablers and barriers to practice HCD-based digitalisation
  • Three types of collaboration approaches in developing digital infrastructures
  • Pathways to successful skill sharing and knowledge exchange in design process
  • Effective experimental and iteration methods to achieve collective innovation
  • The importance of governmental guidance in directing the general goal of digitalisation 

I am currently developing a System Map of collection digitalisation using kumu.io. It will show the interdependence between various factors and actors related to human-centred approach in technology integration.
