Project Summary

The Institute for Digital Culture is a pioneering research centre in helping cultural sectors adapt to digital transformation. They planned to establish research hubs in different regions. I acted as a research assistant to pilot a study that paved the way for the first hub, the "Digital Culture Observatory". It reviewed existing research strategies on digital activities in cultural institutions, and envisioned a series of future-oriented policies and strategies.

Goal

To review the current landscape of cross-sectoral and cross-institutional research collaborations within cultural institutions. As one of the Institute's first research projects with their partners, the goal is to pave the way for establishing the Observatory - a research hub dedicated to digital culture in Australia.

My Role
  • Strategic analysis & development
  • Literature review 

Methods

Scoping Review

I led a review of research strategies related to technology R&D in 30+ cultural institutions, examining their outputs and impact. Using insights garnered from this exploration, the team formulated practical guidelines for both practice and action research tailored to cultural institutions in Australia.

Key Insights

Based on the scoping review, I developed a taxonomy of human-centred research strategies:

  • Practice-based Research fosters co-creation and experimentation in museums.
  • Applied Research uses evidence-informed evaluation to drive the development of impactful solutions, creating tangible benefits for individuals and communities in the long term.
  • Participatory Research promotes community engagement and social change. It is the fundation of human-centred research .
  • Innovative Research Translation challenges conventional research strategies. Using multisensory technologies as creative mediums, it expands the accessibility and reach of research outcomes.

Impact 

The research supported Monash University and the Australian National University in curating a seminar series that built digital research capacity. It initiated partnerships between the Institute for Digital Culture and Australian cultural sectors.
