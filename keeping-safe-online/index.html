Keeping Safe Online

Han Jiang: Design Research & Strategy

Summary

A 4-week research project that developed practical guidance for cultural organisation workers to engage in social media activism, addressing challenges such as misinformation, hate speech, and censorship.

Role

  • Rapid review
  • Workshop facilitation
  • Qualitative analysis

Challenges

The culture sector faces an urgent need to provide guidance on engaging in meaningful online activism while ensuring individual safety, institutional integrity, and compliance with regulations like the controversial Online Safety Bill draft developed in 2021.

Goals

The research answered these questions:

  • How do museum professionals experience risks and rewards of engaging in activism on social media? How do they ensure online safety?
  • What responsibilities do institutions and sectors have in addressing these issues?

Methods

Rapid literature review: I led a rapid review of current sectoral and organisational policy on social media use. 

Focus group workshops: 7 cultural organisation professionals and 6 cross-disciplinary researchers were recruited for co-creation activities in 2 online workshops:

  • Workshop 1: To brainstorm with cultural organisation workers to identify pain points in their social media engagement
  • Workshop 2: To lead researchers explore solutions to these challenges and risks

Triangulation: Key themes extracted from the interviews were triangulated with rapid review findings, and analysed in the report.

Key Insights

  • There has been no systematic support to help museum professionals to reflect on the risks and rewards of using social media at work.
  • Benefits of social media engagement: improving inclusivity and accessibility, promoting workers’ career profile, and building mutual trust between museums and communities. 
  • Harms of social media engagement:
    • Self-censorship: Feeling the need to suppress cultural workers' own opinions or ideas due to fear of public backlash.
    • Mental health issues: Experiencing stress, anxiety, or other mental health challenges, such as cyberbullying or negative online interactions.
    • Blurring of institutional and individual boundaries: Difficulty separating personal views from those of the cultural organisation, leading to potential conflicts or misunderstandings.
