Design Heuristics: FeelTheArt®

Han Jiang: Design Research & Strategy

Summary

FeelTheArt is an art education app developed by Livdeo. It designed to enable users to create personalised online and AR exhibitions using artworks from global cultural institutions. 

Role

  • Heuristic analysis

Process

Heuristics principles development

I developed a series of heuristics principles based on Nielsen's 10 principles for user interface design. In particular, I extended them to include principles regarding social interaction mechanisms and personalisation mechanisms.

Evaluation
After evaluating the experience of landing page, artist profile, gallery curation page, AR creation page, I identified 10 usability issues and suggested areas of improvements. I presented my findings to the development team.

 

Insights

Features are relatively easy to recognise and to learn. The app has potential to enhance its sociality and user control in curating and sharing exhibitions.

To enhance social interactions 

  • Add “swiping” for users to view random galleries curated by other users
  • In the description of the painting featured on the banner, add recommended user-created AR exhibitions/curated galleries featuring the painting 
  • Allow users to post comments under user-curated galleries

To enhance user control and freedom

  • Allow users to add personalised descriptions of artworks in their exhibitions 
  • Develop different templates of exhibitions to give more freedom in interaction (e.g. a whiteboard-based exhibition might be more explorable)

Impact

This evaluation helped Livedeo to improve usability issues in their Beta version.
The app adds a new landing page to offer more effective guidance, links to other curated exhibitions in audio guide pages. Editor selected galleries and content were moved to the top of all user-generated galleries to highlight themes of the artworks.
