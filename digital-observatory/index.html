Transnational Education Partnerships

Han Jiang: Design Research & Strategy

Project Summary

The School of Museum Studies is the leading curatorial research centre in the UK. They want to expand their partnerships with universities in Asia. This project is a pilot study to understand how education partnerships form between universities and cultural organisations in Western and developing countries. I analysed how transnational curatorial education develop within emerging cultural centres, particularly in China, Qatar, and Saudi Arabia. 

Goal

Map out the landscape of transnational education in 3 months of project cycle. Develop a report that offers adivce on partnership research and development.

My Role
  • In-depth interviews & analysis
  • Policy research
  • Report writing 

Methods

Policy Review

I started by developing a spreadsheet covering current higher education programmes about museums and heritage at branch campuses of Western universities. I analysed institutional policy documents relating to their partners and course development.

Semi-structured Interview

From 30 education programmes analysed, I identified 7 case studies and conducted 8 semi-structured interviews with selected participants. This informed strategies for their future partnership-building.

Key Insights

  • Three types of transnational partnerships identified: branch campus, joint degree programmes, and professional training programmes.
  • Transnational education strengthened partnerships between universities, as well as between universities and local museums.
  • Local organisations had limited ownership of education programmes because most of the curriculum development was led by the Western institutions.
  • The pandemic, miscommunications, and different organisational cultures and policies posed significant challenges in partnership management.

Impact

We delivered a report that presented the landscape of transnational collaborations between universities, governments and cultural organisations in China and the Middle East.

Our report was featuerd in a panel discussion at the Anthropology and Education Conference 2024 (Royal Anthropological Institute, London). Our findings secured a funding for future development of the project.
